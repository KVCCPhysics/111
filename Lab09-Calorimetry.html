Experiment 1: Specific Heat

Perform this procedure for each of the metal blocks:

  1. Arrange the styrofoam calorimiter cups in a pair with one cup inside of the other. This insulated (isolated) system will be used as a calorimeter.
  2. Measure the mass of one of the metal blocks and add enough water to cover the metal completely. Make a measurement of the temperature of the water in the calorimeter in the table below.
  3. Take the metal block and carefully place it in the beaker full of boiling water. The Zeroth law of thermodynamics dicatates that the metal block will come into equilibrium with the water at boiling temperature.
  4. Use tongs to quickly remove the metal block from the boiling water and place it into the filled calorimeter.
  5. Record the final temperature of the system after reaching equilibrium.
