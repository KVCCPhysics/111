The purpose of this laboratory is to examine conservation of momentum and energy in a single dimension. You will have two carts on an air track collide and examine if and when momentum and energy are conserved.

Procedure

Important: The carts and tracks are precisely manufactured to have very small friction. This makes the system fragile. Please be careful with the carts and the equipment in this lab!

There are two ends to the air track carts, one creates an elastic collision (end with rubber bands) and one creates a perfectly inelastic collision (ends with pin and pin catcher). You will need to perform 6 different trials in this experiment. For each trial calculate the total incoming momentum and kinetic energy and the outgoing momentum and kinetic energy.

\[\vec{p} = m \vec{v} \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad KE = \frac12 m v^2\]

Note: When performing the recorded experiment, the velocity of the second cart should be zero (the moving cart collides with the stationary cart). You are encouraged to experiment with collisions where the initial conditions have been changed to test your understanding and intuition of collisions.
